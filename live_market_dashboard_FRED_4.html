Integrated signal analysis for your SPY PMCC
VIX elevated — short call should be 7–9% OTM, 14 DTE
With VIX above 30, IV is elevated and GEX is negative. Bear rallies of 2–3% can occur overnight in negative gamma territory. Wide strikes protect against gap-through risk. Short duration (14 DTE) limits your exposure window to any single catalyst. Never hold a short call through a major macro event in this environment.
HY OAS widening — debit put spread beats naked put
A debit put spread (buy SPX 4–5% OTM, sell 200pts lower) costs ~$1,700/cycle vs $7,500 for a naked put — a 77% cost reduction. In the likely flat-to-moderate-down scenario, the spread saves $5,800/cycle while still covering the 4–8% correction range your PMCC is most exposed to. Naked put only wins if SPX crashes more than 250pts below your long strike.
Negative GEX — do not chase bear rallies
Every rally in negative gamma territory stalls at call walls (6,900 / 7,000 SPX). Do not roll your short call up on day 1 of any rally — wait 2 sessions to confirm whether the move holds above Zero Gamma (~6,750 SPX / ~$675 SPY). If it doesn't hold, the short call returns to safe distance naturally.
IG spreads still tight — correction, not recession confirmed
Investment grade at multi-decade tights is the single most important structural green light. Your LEAPS retains structural value. A true recession drawdown requires IG to break above 120bps AND payrolls to go negative. Neither has triggered. The PMCC structure itself is sound — you're managing risk inside a correction, not restructuring for a multi-year bear market.
